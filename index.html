The outsourcing of agricultural production is contributing to biodiversity loss. The increase of importing and exporting food or timber leads to the increase of deforestation, and therefore destroys the habitats of many forest-dependent animals, especially endangered species.

Food waste is a significant contributor to the production of greenhouse gases. Carbon Dioxide is emitted when food is produced, transported, and handled; when its waste is taken to landfills, methane is also emitted.

Fewer than 10 percent of plastics are recycled, while approximately 79 percent end up in landfills. The production and disposal of plastic contribute to greenhouse gas emissions at every stage—from extraction and transportation to refining, waste management, and leakage.

Land degradation poses a major threat to sustainable development, biodiversity conservation, and mitigation and adaptation to climate change. Degraded land can release soil carbon and nitrous oxide into the atmosphere, making land degradation one of the largest contributors to climate change.

The fashion industry, particularly fast fashion, is the second-largest consumer of water. Producing a single cotton shirt requires 700 gallons of water, while making a pair of jeans uses 2,000 gallons, highlighting the enormous amounts of water and energy fast fashion consumes.

The primary driver of global warming is the emissions from fossil fuels. Fossil fuels account for around 80% of the world's energy, and in 2018, the fossil fuel industry was responsible for 89% of global Carbon Dioxide emissions.
